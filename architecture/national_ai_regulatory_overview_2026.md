# AI Projects in Ghana — Regulatory Overview (2026)

This document provides a regulatory perspective on the deployment, ethical validation, and compliance auditing of Artificial Intelligence initiatives across the Republic of Ghana. Designed for IT Regulators, M&E Officers, and Ministry Auditors, it outlines the key frameworks under the forthcoming **Emerging Technologies Bill**, **Data Protection Act (Act 843)**, and **Cybersecurity Act (Act 1038)**.

---

## 🏛️ 1. National Government-Led AI Initiatives

### A. Ghana National AI Strategy (2025–2035)
Launched to govern a ten-year roadmap, establishing a responsible, human-centred AI ecosystem. It mandates that all public and private AI projects must align with Ghanaian ethics, cultural values, and local language accessibility.

### B. National AI Fund
A sovereign financing facility capitalized at **GH₵5 billion** for 2025–2030 (scaling to **GH₵15 billion** for 2030–2035). Funded in part by mineral and oil royalties, it supports start-up acceleration, local research grants, and compute node enhancements.

### C. AI Computing Centre
A **$270 million** high-performance hardware facility ($250 million capital grant + $20 million development partner support) establishing centralized GPU clusters for West Africa. Located in Kumasi, it handles heavy deep learning neural network training workloads.

### D. One Million Coders Programme (OMCP)
A nationwide training initiative spanning **130 learning centres** in all 16 regions. Designed to equip one million citizens in software development, AI engineering, data analytics, and cybersecurity.

### E. Emerging Technologies Bill
Forthcoming legislation setting the primary legal criteria for AI risk categorization, model registration, audit trails, and biometric data uses.

### F. Responsible AI Authority
The central administrative body designed to enforce ethical standards, audit algorithm fairness, verify model cards, and issue compliance certificates.

## 🏥 2. Healthcare AI Sector

* **WHO-UNDP AI Health Programme**: Collaborating with international development partners to harness diagnostic tools, manage epidemiologic trends, and protect vulnerable populations in rural Northern districts.
* **AiCARE**: Diagnostic assistant suite leveraging deep Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) to accelerate radiographic scans.
* **BawaHealth**: Startup machine learning model forecasting clinic pharmaceutical demand and preventing supply out-of-stocks.

---

## 🌾 3. Agriculture AI Sector

* **Sesi Technologies / AI Agritech**: Deep learning computer vision platform scanning crop and leaf conditions to detect infections and predict cocoa harvest yields in the Western North region.

---

## 💰 4. Fintech & Financial AI Sector

* **Oze**: Cash flow prediction and transaction bookkeeping automation engine for small businesses.
* **KudiGO**: Retail store sales predictor and inventory manager.
* **Alternative Credit Scoring**: Alternative credit rating system matching mobile money patterns with transaction history weights to offer financing access to unbanked populations.

---

## 📋 5. Regulatory & Compliance AI

* **Regulon**: Compliance risk assessor selected in the 2025 Google for Startups Accelerator. Automates corporate onboarding audits and verifies data protection compliance.

---

## 🗣️ 6. Language & NLP AI

* **Khaya AI / Ghana NLP**: Multilingual speech translation engine translating local low-resource Ghanaian languages (Twi, Fante, Ga, Ewe) to facilitate digital access.

---

## 🎓 7. Education AI Sector

* **Ghana Code Club**: Foundational digital literacy and coding club training schools in Tarkwa and rural Western hubs.
* **DOBIISON Ghana Ltd**: Immersive virtual reality tutorials and STEM study aids equipped with simple ML tutors.
* **Literacy For Life**: Translation notepad and speech tracker aiding underserved Northern communities.

---

## 🤝 8. Data, Partnerships & PPPs

* **DataNexus AI & Bloop Global**: Relational data warehousing and supply-chain logistics tracking hubs using ML analytics.
* **SoftTribe**: Voice-enabled utility customer service chatbots.
* **MTN MoU**: Public-private digital infrastructure design partnership.
* **UNESCO & British High Commission**: Regulatory study tour to the United Kingdom to benchmark AI governance architectures.
* **IMF / World Bank / AfDB Collaboration**: Multilateral funding partnerships support regional data center deployments.

> **IT Regulator Compliance Mandate:** All systems registered above are subject to the **National Governance Score (NGS)** audit under the Responsible AI Authority. Systems classified as Critical Information Infrastructure (CII) under **Act 1038** must maintain immutable append-only transaction logs. High-risk systems must submit to semi-annual penetration testing logs.
